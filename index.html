  • ‚Ä¢ Aromatherapy: Releases a distinct, calming scent when wet.

Toplunge Engineering Note: We only use "Clear Grade A" cedar. Cheaper competitors use "Knotty Grade," where knots can dry out and fall out, creating leaks.

2. Pine & Hemlock (Budget Trap)

High Risk

Many manufacturers push these woods because they are cheap and easy to machine. For a dry sauna? Excellent. For a wet cold plunge? A disaster waiting to happen.

Why they fail in Ice Baths:

  • Resin Leakage (Pine): When Pine gets hot (outdoors), it bleeds sticky resin. It‚Äôs ugly, ruins clothes, and clogs chiller filters.
  • The "Sponge" Effect (Hemlock): Hemlock has almost no natural rot resistance. Without a heavy chemical coating (varnish), it absorbs condensation, turns black, and rots within a year.

The "Secret" to Longevity: The Liner

Stop worrying about the wood holding the water. The wood should only be the skin. The skeleton must be stainless steel or heavy-duty plastic. This is the Toplunge Standard.

Wood Only

Relies on wood swelling to seal gaps. High leak risk. High maintenance. Not recommended.

316 Stainless

The Toplunge Gold Standard. 316-grade steel resists salt and chemical corrosion. Easy to clean. Zero leaks.

Acrylic/Drop-in

Good durability, but can scratch. Ensure the wood wrapping it is ventilated to prevent trapped moisture.

Which Wood Fits Your Climate?

Hot & Dry (Arizona/Nevada)

Wood shrinks in dry heat.

  • Best: Thermo Wood (Heat treated to be stable).
  • Avoid: Pine (Sap will bleed).

High Humidity (Florida/SE Asia)

Mold is the enemy here.

  • Best: Red Cedar (Natural fungicide) or WPC.
  • Avoid: Hemlock (Will grow black mold).

Freezing (Canada/Nordics)

Freeze/thaw cycles crack wood.

  • Best: Toplunge Composite (WPC) or Thick Cedar.
  • Avoid: Thin varnish coatings.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I stop my Cedar tub from leaking red water?

This is called "Tannin Bleeding." It is natural. Simply fill the tub, let it sit for 24 hours, and drain. Repeat this process 2-3 times during the first week. The tannins are harmless but can stain concrete.

Q: How often should I oil the wood?

For Red Cedar or Thermo Wood kept outdoors, we recommend applying a UV-resistant timber oil (like Osmo or Tung Oil) every 6 months to maintain the rich color. Without oil, all wood will eventually turn silver-gray.

Q: Why does Toplunge recommend Stainless Steel liners?

Wood expands when wet and shrinks when dry. In a cold plunge, this movement causes leaks. A stainless steel liner (304 or 316 grade) ensures the tub is 100% waterproof regardless of the wood's movement.
